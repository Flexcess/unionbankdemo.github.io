INFLATION INDEXED NATIONAL SAVING SECURITIES- CUMULATIVE (IINSS-C)

Overview

As per Notification F.No.4 (16) W&M/2012 dated December 19, 2013, the Government of India has announced issuance of “Inflation Index National Savings securities-Cumulative, 2013” (“the Bonds”) with effect from December 23, 2013 to December 31, 2013.Now, This scheme has been extended till 31 March,2014. However,the Government of India reserves the right to close the issue earlier than March 31, 2014.Our bank is one of the authorized banks to receive application and subscription from the investors whether he is our customer or walking client.The terms and conditions and features of this issue of the bonds as per the above referred notification in short are as follows-

Eligible Investors

The eligible investors for these bonds would include individuals not being a Non- Resident Indian , Hindu Undivided Family (HUF), charitable institutions registered under section 25 of the Indian Companies Act and Universities incorporated by Central, State or Provincial Act or declared to be a university under section 3 of the University Grants Commission Act, 1956 (3 of 1956). The above proposed eligible investors in these bonds are the same as in the Relief/Savings Bonds.

Product Features
  • Face value of one security - ` 5,000 (Rupee five thousand) and minimum investment - ` 5,000 (Rupee five thousand).
  • Maximum investment - ` 500,000 (Rupee five lakh) per applicant per annum.
  • Rate of interest (per annum) –real interest rate (fixed rate) + inflation rate calculated with respect to final combined Consumer Price Index [(CPI) Base;2010=100].
  • Real interest rate – 1.5% per annum and the same will act as floor.
  • Compounding - Half-yearly
  • Tenor - 10 years.
  • nflation- final combined CPI will be used as reference CPI with a lag of three months (i.e. final combined CPI for September 2013 would be reference CPI for all days of December 2013). In case of change in the base year, the base splicing method will be used.
  • Early Redemption - after one year from date of issue for senior citizens above 65 years of age and 3 years for all others. The penalty charges at the rate of 50% of the last coupon payable for early redemption. Early redemptions to be allowed only on coupon dates.
  • Nomination - A sole holder or a sole surviving holder of these bonds, being an individual, may nominate one or more persons who shall be entitled to the bonds and the payment thereon in the event of his death. The Non-Resident Indians (NRIs) can also be nominee of these bonds.
